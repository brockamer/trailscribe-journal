Temp dropped faster than expected once the sun went down. That coastal wind off the Pacific cuts right through a fleece. We huddled close, passed something warm to drink, and didn't talk much — just watched the sky go fully dark. No marine layer tonight, which almost never happens. You could see the channel islands as a faint silhouette. Nights like this remind you why people come out here at all.
