cables slick with ice
forty miles of wind finds bone
ridge holds nothing back

Gusts hit forty at the top, no exaggeration. The cables were glazed over, hands working through gloves to find any grip. Fourteen degrees and the wind had a mean edge to it, the kind that finds every gap in your layers. Moved slow and deliberate across the exposed section. Sky was partly open, sun giving light but no warmth. Below the pass the air went quiet fast. Legs still have something left. Camp when I find shelter.
