Pink fades into grey
fog swallows the frozen lake
one star holds the dark

Sunset came and went fast. The fog rolled in off the water before the last color even cleared the ridge, muting everything to a flat pewter. At 19 degrees the air bites through any gap in the layers. The lake surface looks locked solid near the inlet. Hard to tell where ice ends and fog begins. Stayed just long enough to watch one star punch through the overcast, then turned back toward camp before full dark took the trail.
