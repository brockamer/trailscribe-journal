purple holds the cold
scree spills down the shadowed face
still air, still flowers

Crested the ridge and dropped into a lupine meadow I hadn't expected at this elevation. At 14°F the blooms looked improbable, dense purple against pale granite. The north face beyond was loose scree top to bottom — slow going, each step sliding back a few inches. Wind stayed light. Partly cloudy kept the light flat, which made the meadow color punch harder. Worth noting for a return trip when the snow is fully off.
