Caught the alpenglow just after waking. The east face of Whitney went pink before anything else moved — granite holding the color longer than I expected, almost orange near the summit blocks. Fourteen degrees and the wind was steady enough to feel through the shell. Hands cold on the camera. The partly cloudy sky kept the light honest, no drama, just stone doing what stone does at first light.
