knee-deep and rushing
ice-edged stones shift underfoot
the far bank holds still

Water was higher than expected at the Kearsarge crossing. Knee-deep, fast enough to pull, and cold enough to ache through the boots inside of ten seconds. Picked my line carefully — upstream of the usual ford, where the channel spreads a little. Made it across without going down, but it was close. Hands shaking on the trekking poles after. Air at 14°F doesn't help you dry out fast. Marked the crossing as sketchy for anyone following.
