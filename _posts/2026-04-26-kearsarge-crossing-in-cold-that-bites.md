Knee-deep, cold water
stones shift beneath numb bare feet
far bank holds its breath

The crossing was sketchier than expected. Water ran knee-deep and fast, cold enough to pull the breath right out of me. Footing was uncertain — each step a small negotiation with the current. Made it across but stood on the far bank a minute longer than necessary, letting the feeling come back into my feet. At 14°F the wet didn't forgive quickly. Filed it away: next time, earlier in the day, trekking poles ready before stepping in.
