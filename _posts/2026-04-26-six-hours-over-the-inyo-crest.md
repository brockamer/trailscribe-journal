Left the trailhead before first light with maybe six usable hours before the next system pushes in from the west. Fourteen degrees and a steady twelve-mile wind off the crest. The sky is holding for now — partly cloudy, stars still visible at the margins. Moving fast on frozen scree, which is easier than loose rock but demands attention. Nothing to linger over today. This is a weather-window run, not a wander. Get up, get the data, get back before the grey wall closes.
