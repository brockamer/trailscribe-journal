scree stacked against the north wall
wind cuts without pause

Crested the ridge and dropped into a lupine meadow sitting right at eleven thousand. Didn't expect color up here, not with the north face still buried in scree and the temperature barely above single digits. The flowers looked wrong for the cold—too vivid, too insistent. Wind out of the northwest, steady, nothing dramatic. The scree field runs steep and loose above the treeline. Footing was honest work. No other tracks in the frost.
