Ran the ridge at first light with the smoke already pooling in the valley floor. Eyes started watering before mile two. The cold kept the haze low, trapped beneath the inversion layer, and the Sierra walls held it there like a bowl. Stopped twice to let the burning ease. Partly cloudy kept the sun diffuse, which helped some. Smell of char carried on the eleven-mile wind. Hard to tell where the fire is from here.
