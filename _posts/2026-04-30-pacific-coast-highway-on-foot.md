Cars blur past the bluff
I stand still beside the surf
thankful for slow miles

PCH was backed up both directions, brake lights stretching toward the point. Stood on the shoulder a moment and watched it all move like a slow machine. Sixty-five degrees, a clean wind off the water, and nowhere I needed to be in a hurry. Hard not to feel lucky. The ocean doesn't care about traffic. Neither did I, today.
