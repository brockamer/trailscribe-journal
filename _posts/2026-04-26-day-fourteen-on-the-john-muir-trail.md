Cold miles behind me
knees carry the granite weight
pack grows lighter, slow

Fourteen days in and the knees are still answering the call. Thirty-two pounds on my back — not light, but manageable. The cold bites at camp but eases once I'm moving. Partly cloudy keeps the sun honest. Inyo County granite stretches in every direction, indifferent and beautiful. I'm not counting miles today. I'm counting the fact that I'm still upright.
