twenty-three degrees and still
wheels trace the shoreline

Departed Malibu under a clear morning sky, temperature sitting at a comfortable 66°F with a steady 14-mile-per-hour breeze off the water. The air had that particular coastal quality — dry enough to feel clean, salted enough to remind you exactly where you are. No marine layer to speak of, which meant unobstructed sightlines across the Pacific from the first mile out.

The route ran point-to-point and looped back, covering 100.40 kilometers over 475 minutes — just under eight hours behind the wheel. Elevation gain came in at 782 meters across the full distance, modest but present, accumulated in the rolls and switchbacks that define this stretch of Southern California coastline. The terrain climbs away from sea level quickly once you leave the shore corridor, threading through chaparral-covered ridgelines before descending again toward the coast.

Average speed held at 46.3 km/h across the session, which speaks to the nature of the road itself — narrow lanes, tight curves, occasional stops, and sections where the pavement demands your full attention. The p95 speed reaching 110.5 km/h indicates the handful of open stretches where the road straightens and the view opens wide, inviting a longer gear and a moment of velocity before the next bend pulls you back down.

A long pause mid-route allowed time to step out and take in the ridge view — the kind of stop that doesn't register as a destination but becomes one anyway. The wind at elevation was sharper than at the coast, pushing through the scrub oak and manzanita with a dry rustling that carried no moisture. The sky held its clarity the entire day, no clouds building inland, no haze thickening over the water.

The return to Malibu closed the loop in late afternoon light, the sun dropping toward the horizon at an angle that turned the coastal bluffs amber. Total elevation gain and the varied pace tell the story of a route that is never fully relaxed and never fully demanding — it asks for attention throughout, rewards it in equal measure, and ends where it began with the ocean still in view.
