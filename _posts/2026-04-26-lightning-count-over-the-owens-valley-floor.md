thunder rolls the basin wide
storm still far enough

Cold enough that breath comes out in clouds. Stopped mid-stride on the run to watch lightning fork over the valley — counting Mississippis out of habit, the way you do as a kid. Storm sitting well offshore to the east, thunder arriving slow and low across the flat. Fourteen degrees and the air feels electric anyway, dry and sharp. Kept the pace easy after that, one eye on the sky. The Inyos don't give much warning.
