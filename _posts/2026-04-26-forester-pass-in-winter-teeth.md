cables slick with ice
wind tears words from open mouths
step and do not stop

Gusts hit forty on the ridge before the cables. Ice had built up overnight — not thick, but enough to matter underfoot. Hands stayed on the line the whole traverse. Sky partly open, sun offering light but no warmth at fourteen degrees. Wind came in pulses, the kind that lean you sideways if you're not braced. Moved through without stopping. Below the pass the air settled and the legs remembered what they were for.
