cold granite turns pink
first light holds the frozen peak
then the color dies

Caught the alpenglow just after waking. The whole east face of Whitney went pink-orange before I'd even pulled on my boots — that particular shade granite gets when the sun is low and the air is clear and cold. Fourteen degrees. Fingers stiff working the laces. The color held maybe three minutes, then the clouds shifted and it was just grey rock again. Worth the early rise.
