Departed Brownsville, Tennessee under a clear morning sky, the temperature already sitting at a comfortable 72°F with a light 6 mph breeze doing little more than nudging the grass along the highway shoulders. The land here is low and broad — west Tennessee cotton country giving way to the floodplain margins of the Mississippi — and the road reflects that geography without apology. Flat, straight, and purposeful.

The first stretch out of Brownsville runs west on US-70, a corridor that has been carrying traffic toward the river for well over a century. The pavement was dry and clean, visibility unlimited. Average speed settled around 88 km/h for much of the Tennessee segment, the kind of pace that lets you register the landscape without losing it entirely — grain elevators, catfish ponds, the occasional roadside stand shuttered until a later hour.

Crossing the Mississippi is never incidental, regardless of how many times the bridge appears in the windshield. The I-40 bridge at Memphis spans the river at a height that briefly suspends the sense of ground altogether. The water below was wide and brown and moving with its characteristic indifference. West Memphis, Arkansas arrived quickly on the far bank, and the character of the drive shifted — the Delta floodplain continuing, but the light somehow different, the horizon a degree wider.

The I-40 westbound corridor through central Arkansas is an efficient piece of road. Traffic was light under the clear sky, and the p95 speed of 129 km/h reflects the occasional open stretch where the road widened and the miles compressed. Elevation gain across the entire 331-kilometer run was a negligible 31 meters — a number that confirms what the eye already suspected: this is some of the flattest driving in the mid-South.

The approach to Maumelle comes as the Arkansas River draws close on the south side of the interstate, the bluffs of the Little Rock area beginning to suggest themselves on the horizon — the first real topographic relief of the journey. The drive concluded in 274 minutes, a clean point-to-point run with no significant delays, the clear weather holding from start to finish.
