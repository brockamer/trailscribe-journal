cold rings on still glass
trout lips kiss the fading light
frost waits for morning

Watched them rise for nearly an hour as the sun dropped behind the ridge. Consistent rings near the inlet, a few larger fish working the far edge. Tied on a few candidates by headlamp — nothing decided yet. Temperature dropped hard after dark. Fingers stiff by the time I got back to camp. Twelve degrees tonight means a slow start tomorrow, but the fish were active at last light and I expect they will be again.
