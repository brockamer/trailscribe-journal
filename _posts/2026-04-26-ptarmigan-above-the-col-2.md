white birds on white snow
only the dark eyes betray
what the cold conceals

Crossed the col on run 11. Wind was steady out of the northwest, cold enough to keep the snow dry and loose underfoot. Three ptarmigan held tight against a boulder field just above the saddle, their winter plumage nearly perfect against the slope. Spotted them only because one turned its head. They didn't flush. Stood there like they owned the ridge, which I suppose they do.
